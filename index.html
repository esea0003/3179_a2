Life Expectancy Around the World

A visualisation by Edelyn Seah

This study endeavors to bridge existing research gaps by utilizing a mixed-effects regression model, drawing upon data spanning from 2000 to 2015. It incorporates essential immunisation metrics, including Hepatitis B, Polio, and Diphtheria, and systematically examines a range of variables, such as mortality rates, economic factors, sociocultural influences, and healthcare indicators. By conducting a cross-country analysis, our research aims to pinpoint the precise determinants contributing to decreased life expectancy. This analysis can serve as a valuable resource for nations seeking to refine their public health strategies and prioritize areas of intervention to enhance the well-being of their populations.

Infant Deaths Across all Countries in 2015

High infant mortality rates are a key contributor to lower overall life expectancy. The following graphs will highlight the role of immunisation, specifically against diseases like Hepatitis B, Polio, and Diphtheria, in reducing infant deaths. immunisation helps protect infants from life-threatening diseases, which, in turn, can significantly boost life expectancy by reducing the burden of infant mortality.

This shows the infant mortality rates across all countries. We see that India has the highest number mortality rates of 1800 deaths in 2015. India may experience high infant mortality rates due to factors such as inadequate access to healthcare, limited prenatal care, and a higher prevalence of poverty, malnutrition, and preventable diseases.

Highest Mortality Rates

High adult mortality rates indicate a population's susceptibility to premature death during adulthood, often due to factors like chronic diseases, limited healthcare access, lifestyle choices, and socioeconomic disparities. Addressing these factors is crucial for improving overall life expectancy and public health.

Based on the chart, we can observe that Lesotho has the highest adult mortality rates of 484 deaths per year by 2015. It is also important to note that in 2010, Lesotho is still ranked 2nd. Similarly to India, challenges such as malnutrition, high rates of poverty and lack of knowledge of vaccines could be the cause of high adult mortality rates.

Life Expectancy Rates in 2005 vs 2015

Between 2005 and 2015, the life expectancy rates might vary, reflecting improved healthcare, disease control, and overall public health. This incline signifies significant progress in enhancing population well-being.

Developed countries like Singapore, Australia, Germany, and Luxembourg tend to have higher life expectancy rates compared to developing nations such as India, the Democratic Republic of Congo, Uganda, and Nigeria. This difference is mainly due to better healthcare systems, higher living standards, and more effective public health efforts in developed countries. Developing nations face challenges like limited healthcare access and higher disease burdens, which contribute to lower life expectancy.

Diphtheria and Polio Immunisation Rates

Immunisation against Diphtheria and Polio diseases helps create herd immunity, making it difficult for the diseases to spread within the population. By preventing outbreaks of Diphtheria and Polio, immunisation lowers mortality rates, especially among vulnerable groups such as children and the elderly. High immunisation coverage is a crucial component of public health efforts to minimize preventable deaths from these diseases.

Developed countries typically exhibit high immunization rates, thanks to well-established healthcare systems, robust public health programs, and better access to vaccines. In contrast, developing countries may face challenges in achieving high immunization rates due to limited resources, inadequate healthcare infrastructure, and barriers to vaccine distribution.

Years of Schooling on Life Expectancy Rates

Education equips individuals with the knowledge and skills to make informed decisions about their health, access healthcare services, and lead healthier lifestyles. It also fosters economic opportunities, which can improve living conditions and access to better healthcare.

As shown above, populations with higher levels of education tend to have a greater awareness of health practices and a better ability to navigate healthcare systems, ultimately leading to improved life expectancy rates.

Conclusion

In summary, factors such as immunization, healthcare access, and education significantly influence life expectancy. Developed countries tend to have higher immunization rates and healthcare access, contributing to longer lifespans, while developing nations face greater challenges in these areas. Increasing life expectancy over time underscores the potential for improving overall quality of life through better healthcare, access to education, and public health initiatives.
